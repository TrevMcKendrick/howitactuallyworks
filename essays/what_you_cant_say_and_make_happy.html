The Links

The Psychology of Money (collaborativefund.com, article)

20 flaws and biases people have with dealing money. This is not a listicle but a(nother) terrific report from Collaborative Fund.

Hard to excerpt but includes points like:

3) Rich man in the car paradox (not what you think)

5) Anchored-to-your-own-history bias

7) The seduction of pessimism in a world where optimism is the most reasonable stance.

11) The social utility of money coming at the direct expense of growing money

14) A tendency to be influenced by the actions of other people who are playing a different financial game than you are.

The Game of Trust (ncase.me, hard to describe just check it out)

It sure feels like there’s less trust in the US & worldwide right now. Why? Are people just acting worse? Is there some external force?

This uses game theory to help explain what might be going on. (Might only work on desktop but not sure.)

Drew Carey on WTF Podcast (wtfpod.com, audio)

Like many great comedians Carey has a rough life growing up. He also served in the military (I had no idea).

My favorite part was hearing him explain how he did the math on how many jokes he needed to write every day so he could have a full 45-minute show by the end of the year.

What You Can’t Say (paulgraham.com, article)

“Have you ever seen an old photo of yourself and been embarrassed at the way you looked? Did we actually dress like that? We did. And we had no idea how silly we looked. It's the nature of fashion to be invisible, in the same way the movement of the earth is invisible to all of us riding on it.

“What scares me is that there are moral fashions too. They're just as arbitrary, and just as invisible to most people. But they're much more dangerous.”

A classic.

Make Happy (netflix.com, comedy special)

To celebrate its 2-year release anniversary I’m sharing this today. I don’t want to play it up too much but this is likely my single favorite hour of media from the last few years.

Bo Burnham’s writing is easily as good as Lin-Manual Miranda and up there with Bob Dylan. Watch to the end.

I always like hearing feedback, but particularly today if anyone else is a Bo fan or watches Make Happy I’d love to hear what you think.
