Voice vs Exit (video)

Talk that Balaji Srinivasan gave at Y Combinator’s Startup School in 2013.

“I believe that the ability to reduce the importance of decisions made in DC in particular without lobbying or sloganeering is actually going to become extremely important over the next 10 years.”

Why?

Because today it’s Silicon Valley vs “the Paper Belt”: New York, DC, Boston, and Los Angeles. Media, research, law, finance, and culture were all set by these 4 cities, in 19th century institutions that have been fundamentally undermined by the Internet.

The Mormon Church vs. The Internet (article)

“The early years of Mormonism are well-documented and easily examined online. The Internet has also given Mormons new platforms, from forums to podcasts, where they can share their findings. The result has been a mass undoctrination.”

A significant part of the pain in leaving Mormonism is that so much of it is good – the community, the sense of identity, the feeling of belonging to something bigger than yourself – but at bottom it’s based on total nonsense.

The saddest part about Mormonism isn’t any specific action or ugly doctrine, but that every amazing thing it promised you is wrong.
