The Links

The Nerd as the Norm (everythingstudies.com, article)

Psychology Today wrote a “Field Guide to The Nerd”, which a self-declared nerd took a bit of offense to.

So he imagined what if the world was full of nerds and instead the socially active extroverted “jocks” were the exception? What would the “field guide for the anti-nerd” look like?

Some traits of the anti-nerd personality:

• “an interest in people over things and ideas

• a concern for social harmony over correctness

• obliviousness to inconsistency, vagueness and ambiguity”

How Much It Cost to Bribe Individuals in Power in Peru (fermatslibrary.com, pdf)

A paper with detail on how much it cost 1 individual to run the entire democracy of Peru via bribery during the 90’s.

For example, some judges received $2.5k - $10k USD per month, various politicians were paid up to $20k per month to change parties, TV stations were paid up to $9M / year.

I only read half of this (it’s long) but it’s detailed data (the briber took videos of his negotiations and made his counter parties sign contracts) is worth dipping into.

Why some entrepreneurs won’t sell their companies even if it would make them fabulously wealthy (quora.com, article)

Entrepreneur who sold his company for ~$150M to Adobe explains why to some it’s not about money, but the journey, legacy, and glory of building your own thing.
