Choosing Optimism

I’d received some feedback from coworkers last year that I could perhaps be a little more positive. So in early January, before Corona had presented itself as a significant threat, I created a personal slogan for the year.

I called it Optimism2020.

I was so sick of hearing about the presidential election, so I wanted to create my own kind of campaign for 2020.

A campaign to change my own thinking.

I’ve always felt like one needs to be able to justify their optimism. Which is stupid… the whole point of optimism is being able to think and dream beyond whatever your current situation is.

But I wasn’t mentally there yet, so I created Optimism2020 as a personal rallying call that would give me an excuse to be positive.

And now it turns out 2020 is quite the freaking year to start actively choosing to be optimistic. It’s like the pessimist’s nightmare become real… “the one time I try and be positive LOOK WHAT HAPPENS.”

Which honestly makes it the best possible time to increase one’s optimism. What better test for choosing optimism than the biggest worldwide crisis in possibly a hundred years.

To that end, I wanted to make Optimism2020 official today and announce it to the world.

And so I created the below manifesto.

Optimism2020: A Manifesto

Optimism2020 means focusing our time & attention on the things we can influence.

Optimism2020 means doing something today instead of daydreaming about tomorrow.

Optimism2020 means seeing problems as opportunities to learn, grow, and give meaning to our lives.

Optimism2020 means keeping promises to yourself, no matter how small. Telling yourself you’ll go on a walk and then doing it. Promising to call a friend, and doing it. Telling your boss you’ll send that email, and doing it.

Optimism2020 means yes that thing of yours just broke, but believing you’ll be able to figure out how to fix it.

Optimism2020 means carefully choosing the stories you tell yourself about who you are and what you’re capable of.

Optimism2020 means moving fast. Most decisions can be reversed later.

Optimism2020 is someone asking you how you’re doing, and earnestly replying “great!”

Optimism2020 means being you. Not because you’re entitled to be heard but because there is some unique thing you have to offer, if you can find it.

Optimism2020 means believing we can think, and build, and create ourselves out of any problem.

Optimism2020 means knowing that the pie is not fixed, that life is not zero sum.

Optimism2020 means focusing on why something could work instead of why it won’t.

Optimism2020 means seeing yourself as part of the solution, not a victim of the problem.

Optimism2020 means paying for the drive thru order of the car behind you.

Optimism2020 means seeing problems as temporary and specific, not permanent and expansive.

Optimism2020 means believing problems are solvable. Maybe not right away, probably not easily, but that it can be done.
