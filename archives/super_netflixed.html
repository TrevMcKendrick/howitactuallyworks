The Links

Super 30 Trailer (video)

Movie about Anand Kumar, the founder of Super 30.

Super 30 is selects 30 kids from impoverished regions of India and prepares them for the entrance exam to IIT. In 2008, six years after the founding, all 30 kids passed.

How many more kids beyond those 30 could be trained to get into IIT? I wonder if you could build a business around that…

Progress Studies (article)

Patrick Collison and Tyler Cowen describe a plan for us to learn how to improve humanity better.

How to kill a project by vastly underestimating it (article)

“But if you underestimate the resources you need by 3–5x, then when you get close to the end of them, you don’t even see the light at the end of the tunnel. And so you don’t know whether you’re in a tunnel, or just going deeper into a cave with a dead end. You start questioning everything about the project, including whether it’s even the right project.”

Netflixed (book)

Covers the history of Netflix from founding to 2012. It’s so easy to assume that Blockbuster was inevitably going to die, but Netflix was on the ropes more than once.

The biggest successes are literally built, and almost never inevitable.

America’s Bitter Pill (book)

Soup to nuts story on how the Affordable Care Act was passed. Terrifying seeing the chaos that goes into creating, passing, and enacting a bill that affects 20% of the US economy and that no one can opt out of.
